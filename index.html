Who We Are

More Than
Dance

Pynk Ice BTX is Bryan, Texas's rising youth majorette dance team. We're building something extraordinary right here in the Brazos Valley — a sisterhood of young women who move with power, lead with confidence, and show up for each other every single day.

We started at a public park with a dream and a boom box. Every step, every routine, every sequin has been earned through hard work and community love. Our girls practice outdoors, fundraise together, and compete across Texas — and they do it all with pride.

"Cute, confident, and ready to lead." — Pynk Ice BTX
We believe every child who wants to dance deserves the chance.

Majorette dancing is a uniquely American art form rooted in the traditions of Black high school and college bands across the South. Emerging prominently in the early 20th century, majorette culture was born on the fields and streets of Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs) like Southern University, Grambling State, and Florida A&M — institutions that turned halftime into a stage for athletic artistry, cultural expression, and community pride.

At its core, majorette dancing fuses precision drill, baton twirling, hip-hop, jazz, and African movement traditions into something electrifying and deeply communal. The dance captain, the majorette line, and the roar of a crowd responding to a perfectly synchronized routine — these are moments that have shaped Black cultural identity for generations.

In the Black community, majorette dancing represents far more than performance. It is a pipeline for discipline, confidence, and academic achievement. Girls who participate in structured dance programs develop leadership skills, learn to work as a team, and gain access to scholarship opportunities through HBCU band programs. These traditions have produced engineers, doctors, educators, and community leaders who all trace part of their foundation back to the field.

Why it matters today: As funding cuts threaten school band programs and after-school arts face defunding, organizations like Pynk Ice BTX carry the torch. By making majorette dance affordable and accessible right here in Bryan, TX, we ensure that the next generation of young Black girls never loses this inheritance — this legacy of power, beauty, and pride that belongs to them.

The Program

What Our Dancers
Get Out of This

🎭
Performance Training

Majorette technique, showmanship, and stage presence — developed by coaches who invest personally in every dancer's growth.

👑
Leadership Development

From Mini stars to senior captains — every dancer learns what it means to lead from the front. Our Mini program builds confidence early.

🏆
Competition Experience

We travel and compete across Texas. Our girls represent Bryan with pride and come home with skills — and memories — that last a lifetime.

💃
Sisterhood

Beyond routines, Pynk Ice builds real friendships and community. Our dancers show up for each other on and off the floor.

🌟
Character & Discipline

Dance teaches focus, dedication, and teamwork. Our girls carry that energy into school and life — and it shows in everything they do.

📣
Community Presence

We perform at local events, represent Bryan, TX with energy and excellence, and inspire the next generation of dancers.
